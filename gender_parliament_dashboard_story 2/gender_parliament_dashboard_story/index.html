Gender Representation in Pacific Parliaments

Why This Matters

This dashboard visualizes the proportion of women in national parliaments across Pacific countries. Gender parity in political representation is a key target of Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) 5. This dashboard uses historical trends to show how far each nation has progressed, and which still face challenges in including women in legislative processes.

Trend Over Time

This line chart shows the yearly progression of women's parliamentary representation in the selected country. Countries like Marshall Islands show flat trends, indicating stagnation over decades.

Cross-country Comparison

Compare the performance of each country in a specific year. Some, like Palau, have shown significant spikes, while others remain consistently low.

Representation Level

This pie chart illustrates the classification of gender representation (Low, Medium, High) for a selected country and year.

Top 5 Insights from Pacific Representation Data

  1. Persistent low levels: A majority of Pacific nations have had less than 10% female parliamentary participation for decades.
  2. Sporadic improvement: Countries like Palau showed spikes in representation, suggesting event-based or policy-driven shifts.
  3. Flat progression trend: Most nations, such as Marshall Islands, show no significant growth in participation over 20+ years.
  4. Post-2010 momentum: Many shifts upward begin to appear after 2010, potentially reflecting global SDG initiatives.
  5. Policy implications: The data supports a clear need for systemic reforms and affirmative action to drive meaningful progress by 2030.
